Hong Kong Old Style Scoring HK
Scoring is based on Fan 番 (doubles). Identify which patterns your winning hand contains, add up the Fan, and collect from opponents. Most tables require a minimum of 3 Fan to declare a win. Fan values below reflect common Hong Kong practice — exact values vary by region and house rules.
PatternFan
Seat Wind Pung — three of your own seat wind tile+1
Round Wind Pung — three of the current prevailing wind+1
Dragon Pung — three of any dragon (Red, Green, or White)+1
Self-Draw win (Zimo) — you drew your own winning tile from the wall+1
All Concealed — no exposed melds; hand built entirely from your own draws+1
Mixed One-Suit — one number suit plus honor tiles only+1
Last Tile — winning off the very last tile drawn from the wall+1
Dead Wall Draw — winning tile came from after a Kong declaration+1
Robbing a Kong (Chankan) — win off an opponent's Added Kong tile+1
All Pungs — four Pungs (or Kongs) and one Pair; no Chows at all+3
Full Flush — one suit plus honors (a stronger Mixed One-Suit)+3
All Honors — only Wind and Dragon tiles in the entire hand+5
Pure Flush — one suit only; no honor tiles whatsoever+7
How Fan stacking works
Fan is additive across patterns. A self-draw win (+1) with All Pungs (+3) and a Dragon Pung (+1) = 5 Fan total. Fan then multiplies the base point value — a 4-Fan hand is worth twice a 3-Fan hand. Always confirm the base point value and any cap before you start. When East wins, East typically collects double — or all three opponents pay double to East.
Double Wind bonus: If your seat wind matches the round wind — which only happens when you are East during the East Round — a Pung of East Wind scores twice: once for seat wind and once for round wind. That's 2 Fan from a single set.

Japanese Riichi — Essential Yaku Riichi
You must have at least one Yaku 役 to win. No yaku = no win, even with a complete 14-tile hand. Scoring = Han × Fu; at 5+ Han use the mangan thresholds below. Dora tiles add Han but cannot substitute for a Yaku.

American Mahjong — NMJL American

Scoring is card-based. The National Mah Jongg League publishes an annual card listing every valid winning hand, each worth 25 or 50 points. You must match an exact hand from that card — no improvisation, no partial credit. Key differences: Joker tiles (2 per set) substitute for any suit tile in a meld of 3+ but never in a pair. A structured Charleston — three rounds of tile-passing — happens before play begins each hand. Chow sequences are not used. Confirm you have the current year's card before play.

Wind Seats & Seating — Positions rotate each round
Turn order: East (Dealer) → South → West → North → East. East starts with 14 tiles and discards first; all others begin with 13.
Seat Wind vs. Round Wind: Your Seat Wind is your current position. The Round Wind is the prevailing wind for the entire round — East during the East Round, South during the South Round. A Pung of your seat wind = +1 Fan/Han. A Pung of the round wind = another +1 Fan/Han. When you're East in the East Round, one Pung of East earns both — 2 Fan/Han from a single set.
Round structure: One East Round = each player serves as dealer once. A South Round follows. A full game is East + South Rounds minimum; some groups play all four wind rounds.
Dealer Rotation — When the deal passes and when it stays
Hong Kong Style HK
East wins → East stays. A bonus counter is added. When a non-East player wins, all players rotate one seat counter-clockwise — the player who was South becomes the new East. Counter resets.
Japanese Riichi Riichi
Dealer wins or is in tenpai on an exhaustive draw → Dealer stays, honba counter +1. Each honba adds 300 extra points to the next winning hand. If dealer neither wins nor is in tenpai, the deal passes.
American NMJL American
Deal passes to the right after every hand, regardless of who wins. No dealer-retention rule. The Charleston happens at the start of every new deal.
Waiting, Declaring & Penalties
Tenpai 聴牌 Universal
One tile away from winning. When the wall runs out, players in tenpai typically avoid penalties. Declare your tiles face-up to prove tenpai when required by the rules.
Riichi 立直 Riichi Only
Declare tenpai with a closed hand. Place 1,000 points on the table, discard face-down, and lock your hand — no more changes. Win for +1 Han. Unclaimed Riichi bets carry to the next winner.
Furiten 振聴 Riichi Only
If any of your winning tiles appears in your own discard pile, you cannot win by claiming a discard — only by self-draw. Furiten clears when you discard a tile that changes your wait.

Reading the Discard Pile — Basic defensive awareness
Safer Discard Choices
  • Genbutsu: any tile already in a player's own discard pile is 100% safe against them. Riichi
  • Suji: if a player discarded a 4, then 1 and 7 are likely safe — they can't be the center of a 2-3-4 or 4-5-6 wait that needs that 4.
  • Terminals and honors discarded early — often safer late-game since they're hard to build sequences with.
  • Tiles discarded by multiple players — the more copies visible, the fewer waiting hands need them.
Dangerous Discard Choices
  • Middle tiles (4, 5, 6) complete the most possible sequences — most dangerous to throw late in a round.
  • Tiles unseen in any discard — still live in many hands and wallets.
  • Tiles that complete both a sequence and a triplet — dual-use tiles carry double the risk.
  • After a Riichi declaration, study that player's discard history before throwing — they can win off anything in their wait.

On Yakuman
Yakuman hands are the rarest and highest-scoring in Japanese Riichi — equivalent to 13 han (32,000 points for a non-dealer win). They're named, celebrated, and sometimes photographed. In competitive play, some Yakuman hands count as double Yakuman. If you ever build one, show everyone at the table before revealing.

Advanced Glossary — Key terms beyond the basics · dark green = Riichi-specific
TenpaiOne tile away from winning. Declare face-up at exhaustive draw to avoid penalties.
YakuA required scoring pattern in Riichi. No yaku = no win, even with a complete hand.
Kong / KanA set of four identical tiles. Draw one bonus tile from the dead wall after any Kong.
DoraBonus tile adding +1 han per copy in hand. Cannot substitute for a Yaku.
Ura DoraHidden dora revealed only when a Riichi winner wins. Often flips the final score.
FuritenPenalty state: your discard pile contains one of your winning tiles. Cannot win off discards.
IppatsuWin within one full turn cycle of declaring Riichi for +1 han bonus.
Tsumo / RonTsumo = self-draw win. Ron = win by claiming a discard. Both are distinct Yaku in Riichi.
HoujuuDealing into someone's winning hand. In Riichi, the discarder pays the full penalty alone.
Han / FuHan = scoring units from yaku + dora. Fu = sub-unit based on hand structure. Together they determine points.
Mangan5-han threshold in Riichi (8,000 pts non-dealer). Eliminates fu calculation at this level and above.
YakumanMaximum-scoring special hand in Riichi — 32,000 pts non-dealer. Equivalent to 13+ han.
Dead WallLast 14 tiles in the wall (Riichi). Reserved for Kan bonus draws and Dora indicator flips.
SujiDefensive logic: if 4 is discarded, then 1 and 7 are likely safe (can't be the center of a wait that needs that 4).
GenbutsuAny tile already in a player's own discard pile — 100% safe against that player in Riichi.
ChankanClaiming an Added Kong tile to win before the Kong-declarer draws their bonus tile. Rare and dramatic.
HonbaDealer counter in Riichi. Each honba adds 300 pts to the next winning hand; accumulates on dealer stays.
Round WindThe prevailing wind for the current round. East during East Round, South during South Round. Pung of it scores bonus.
Seat WindYour current position's wind (East/South/West/North). Changes each time the deal passes to you.
Fan 番Scoring unit in Hong Kong Mahjong. Patterns are additive; Fan then doubles the base point value.
